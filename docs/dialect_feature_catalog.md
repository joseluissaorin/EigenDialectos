# Dialect Feature Catalog

Comprehensive catalog of the distinguishing features for each of the 8 Spanish varieties modeled in EigenDialectos, organised by linguistic category: lexical, morphosyntactic, pragmatic, and phonological.

---

## ES_PEN: Castellano Peninsular Estandar

**Region:** Central and northern Spain (Madrid, Castilla, Aragon)

### Lexical

- Standard Iberian vocabulary: *ordenador* (computer), *autobus* (bus), *coche* (car), *piso* (apartment), *movil* (mobile phone)
- Food: *patata* (potato), *zumo* (juice), *melocoton* (peach)
- Clothing: *jersey* (sweater), *zapatillas* (sneakers/slippers)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Vosotros** paradigm (2nd person plural): *vosotros habeis visto*, *teneis razon*
- **Leismo** (use of *le* as masculine direct object pronoun): *Le vi ayer* (I saw him)
- Present perfect for recent past: *He comido hoy* (I ate today)
- *Ir a + infinitive* for near future alongside synthetic future

### Pragmatic

- Discourse markers: *vale* (okay), *bueno*, *o sea*, *pues nada*
- Address: *tio/tia* (mate), *colega*
- Intensifiers: *mola* (it's cool), *flipar*, *currar* (to work, colloquial)

### Phonological

- **Distincion**: /theta/ vs /s/ contrast -- *caza* [kaθa] vs *casa* [kasa]
- Full pronunciation of syllable-final /s/: *estos* [estos]
- Interdental /θ/ before /e/, /i/ in orthographic c/z
- Velar fricative /x/: *jugar* [xugar]

## ES_AND: Andaluz

**Region:** Andalusia (Seville, Malaga, Granada, Cadiz, Cordoba)

### Lexical

- Some unique terms: *quillo/quilla* (mate), *picha* (colloquial address), *pringao*
- *Bulla* (hurry), *musho* (mucho)
- Largely shared Iberian vocabulary with Peninsular

### Morphosyntactic

- **Ustedes** replaces *vosotros* in western Andalusia: *Ustedes teneis...*
- Preterite preferred over present perfect: *Comi hoy* (not *He comido*)
- Participle deletion of intervocalic -d-: *cansado* -> *cansao*, *comido* -> *comio*

### Pragmatic

- Discourse markers: *quillo/quilla* (mate), *arsa* (go on!), *picha* (male address), *bah/vah* (dismissive)
- Frequent softening and hedging in speech

### Phonological

- **Seseo** (western/central): /θ/ -> /s/ -- *zapato* [sapato]
- **Ceceo** (southern coastal): /s/ -> /θ/ -- *casa* [kaθa]
- **Aspiration of /s/**: *los ninos* -> [loh ninoh], *esta* -> [ehta]
- **Loss of final consonants**: *verdad* -> *verda*, *Madrid* -> *Madri*
- **Neutralisation of /l/ and /r/**: *alma* ~ *arma*, rotacism *el* -> *er*
- Lenition of intervocalic consonants: *todo* -> [too]
- Opening of preceding vowel upon /s/ aspiration: *estos* [ehtoh]

## ES_CAN: Canario

**Region:** Canary Islands (Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote, Fuerteventura)

### Lexical

- **Guagua** (bus, shared with Caribbean)
- **Papa** (potato, not *patata*)
- Archaisms and Portuguese/Guanche substrate: *machango* (awkward), *gofio* (toasted grain flour)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Ustedes** (no vosotros): *¿Ustedes han visto...?* with 3rd person plural verb forms
- Preterite preferred over present perfect
- Similar to Andalusian and Caribbean morphosyntax

### Pragmatic

- **Chacho/chacha** (mate, from *muchacho/a*): *Chacho, ¿que dices?*
- **Mijo/mija** (my son/daughter, as affectionate address)
- Moderate use of diminutives

### Phonological

- **Seseo**: *zapato* [sapato] (no /θ/)
- Moderate aspiration of syllable-final /s/ (less extreme than Andalusian)
- Retention of intervocalic /d/ more than Andalusian
- Intonation patterns influenced by Portuguese
- Historical bridge between Peninsular and Latin American phonology

## ES_RIO: Rioplatense

**Region:** Buenos Aires, Montevideo, Rosario (Argentina and Uruguay)

### Lexical

- **Lunfardo** vocabulary (Italian, Quechua, Portuguese origin):
  - *pibe/piba* (kid), *laburo* (work), *afanar* (steal), *mango* (peso/money)
  - *bondi* (bus), *birra* (beer), *morfar* (eat)
- *Colectivo* (bus), *departamento* (apartment), *computadora* (computer)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Voseo**: *vos* replaces *tu* with modified verb forms:
  - *Vos tenes*, *vos podes*, *vos hablás*
  - Imperative: *veni*, *decime*, *sentate*
- No *vosotros* (use *ustedes* for plural)
- Preterite strongly preferred over present perfect

### Pragmatic

- **Che** (vocative interjection): *Che, ¿viste lo que paso?*
- **¿Viste?** (tag question, "you see?")
- **Dale** (come on / okay)
- **Boludo/a** (mate, affectionate or pejorative depending on tone)
- **Re** as intensifier: *re bueno* (really good)
- Italian-influenced intonation with rising terminal contours

### Phonological

- **Yeismo rehilado / sheismo**: /ʎ/ and /j/ merge to [ʃ] or [ʒ]:
  - *calle* -> [kaʃe], *lluvia* -> [ʃuβja]
  - Buenos Aires trends toward [ʃ] (voiceless), Montevideo toward [ʒ] (voiced)
- **Seseo** (universal Latin American)
- Syllable-final /s/ generally preserved (not aspirated)

## ES_MEX: Mexicano

**Region:** Mexico (particularly CDMX, central highlands)

### Lexical

- **Nahuatl loanwords**: *aguacate* (avocado), *tomate*, *chocolate*, *chamaco* (kid)
- *Camion* (bus), *computadora* (computer), *departamento* (apartment)
- *Chido/padre/padrisimo* (cool), *chamba* (job), *neta* (truth)
- *Guey/wey* (dude, ubiquitous in informal speech)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Tuteo** (standard *tu*): *Tu tienes...*
- No vosotros; *ustedes* for plural
- Frequent diminutives: *ahorita* (right now), *cerquita* (close by)
- **¿Mande?** instead of *¿como?* or *¿que?* (polite "what?")

### Pragmatic

- **Guey**: universal informal address -- *Orale guey, esta padrisimo*
- **Orale** (wow / come on / okay)
- **¿Va?** (right?), **no manches** (no way), **¿que onda?** (what's up)
- Politeness through diminutives and indirect phrasing

### Phonological

- **Seseo** (universal)
- **Weakening of unstressed vowels**: particularly /e/ and /o/ in rapid speech
- Strong articulation of /s/ (less aspiration than Caribbean/Andalusian)
- Consonant cluster preservation
- Characteristic rhythmic patterns (sometimes called "sing-song")

## ES_CAR: Caribeno

**Region:** Cuba, Puerto Rico, Dominican Republic, coastal Venezuela and Colombia

### Lexical

- *Guagua* (bus, Cuba/PR), *concho/carro publico* (shared taxi, DR)
- *China* (orange, PR), *habichuela* (bean), *boricua* (Puerto Rican)
- *Chevere/cheverisimo* (great), *vaina* (thing, very versatile)
- *Pana* (friend)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Frequent overt pronominal subjects**: *¿Tu fuiste?* (not *¿Fuiste?*)
- *Ustedes* for plural (no vosotros)
- Non-inversion in questions: *¿Que tu quieres?* (instead of *¿Que quieres tu?*)

### Pragmatic

- **Mijo/mija** (my son/daughter): *Mijo, ¿oiste lo que paso?*
- **Oye**, **¿oiste?**, **asere** (buddy, Cuba), **mano/manito** (bro)
- **Pa'** (reduced *para*) in casual speech: *vamos pa' alla*
- Rapid speech tempo with elisions

### Phonological

- **Aspiration and elision of /s/**: *mas o menos* -> *mah o menoh*; extreme loss in final position
- **Lambdacism** (Dominican): /r/ -> /l/ -- *puerta* -> *puelta*, *comer* -> *comel*
- **Velarisation of /n/**: word-final /n/ -> [ŋ] -- *pan* -> [paŋ]
- **Gemination**: /r/ before consonant -> doubling of following consonant: *carne* -> [kanne]
- **Seseo** (universal)
- Intervocalic -d- deletion: *cansado* -> *cansao*

## ES_CHI: Chileno

**Region:** Chile (Santiago, Valparaiso, Concepcion)

### Lexical

- *Fome* (boring), *bacan* (cool), *pololo/polola* (boyfriend/girlfriend)
- *Micro* (bus), *guagua* (baby, not bus), *pega* (job), *luca* (1000 pesos)
- *Al tiro* (right away), *cachar* (to understand/get)
- *Carrete* (party), *chela* (beer)

### Morphosyntactic

- **Informal voseo chileno** (differs from Rioplatense):
  - Pronoun *tu* (not *vos*) but voseante verb endings: *tu tenis*, *tu podis*, *tu sabis*
  - This tuteo-pronominal + voseo-verbal pattern is distinctive to Chilean Spanish
- *Ustedes* for plural (no vosotros)

### Pragmatic

- **¿Cachai?** (you get it?): ubiquitous tag
- **Po** (contraction of *pues*): *si po*, *ya po*, *no po*
- **Hueon/weon** (dude, extremely common in casual speech)
- **La raja** (awesome), **caleta** (a lot)

### Phonological

- **Seseo** (universal)
- **/tʃ/ weakening**: *Chile* -> [ʃile] in casual register
- Aspiration of syllable-final /s/ (moderate, less than Caribbean)
- Weakening of intervocalic /d/: *cansado* -> *cansao*
- Characteristic fast speech tempo with consonant elision

## ES_AND_BO: Andino

**Region:** Highland Peru, Bolivia, Ecuador (Cusco, La Paz, Quito)

### Lexical

- **Quechua/Aymara loanwords**: *choclo* (corn), *cancha* (open field/court), *soroche* (altitude sickness)
- *Chompa* (sweater), *carro* (car), *plata* (money), *chacra* (farm plot)
- Conservative Castilian vocabulary preserved from colonial era

### Morphosyntactic

- **Diminutive overuse**: *ahorita*, *todito*, *cerquita*, *poquitito*
- **Double possessives**: *su casa de Juan*, *su mama de Maria*
- **Dequeismo**: *dice de que...* (insertion of *de* before *que*)
- Past participle as narrative past: *Habia sido su hermano* (meaning "It turned out to be...")
- **Nomas** as attenuative: *pase nomas* (please go ahead)

### Pragmatic

- **Pues / pe**: *Ya pues*, *Entonces pues*, used as filler and connector
- **Nomas**: *Vamos nomas* (just / simply)
- **Ya**: ubiquitous affirmative
- **Taitay/mamita** (respectful address, Quechua influence)
- Conservative and polite register in formal contexts

### Phonological

- **Full pronunciation of /s/**: syllable-final /s/ preserved -- *estos* [estos], *mas* [mas]
- **Partial ll/y distinction**: some speakers maintain /ʎ/ vs /j/ (*calle* vs *cayo*)
- **Quechua vowel influence**: /e/ -> [i] and /o/ -> [u] in contact with velar/uvular consonants
- **Assibilation of /r/**: *perro* -> [peřo] with fricative /r/ in highland regions
- Conservative consonant pronunciation (cluster preservation)
- Strong Quechua/Aymara substrate influence on prosody
